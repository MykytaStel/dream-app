# GO-TO-MARKET.md

## Launch Goal

Launch the app in a way that makes it feel distinctive, desirable, and emotionally memorable.

The goal is not just downloads.
The goal is early users who actually write dreams, return, and talk about the product.

---

## Positioning Strategy

Lead with:
- premium dream journaling
- beautiful and private experience
- fast dream capture
- symbolic and emotional reflection

Possible positioning line:
> A beautiful private space to capture dreams, revisit symbols, and notice what your mind keeps returning to.

---

## Audience to Reach First

Best early audience segments:
- journaling enthusiasts
- lucid dreaming communities
- people interested in reflection and self-awareness
- design-conscious app users
- users dissatisfied with existing dream apps

---

## Launch Assets Needed

- app icon
- screenshots
- app store preview text
- short product story
- landing page or simple waitlist page
- social teaser visuals
- onboarding copy aligned with product tone

---

## Store Strategy

### App Store / Play Listing Focus
Emphasize:
- dream capture speed
- privacy
- beautiful design
- insights and reflection
- offline/local-first trust if true at launch

### Screenshot Themes
- quick capture
- elegant timeline
- emotional tags and symbols
- insight cards
- private lock or trust message

---

## Early Retention Strategy

Keep users through:
- first-entry satisfaction
- reminder quality
- visual delight when revisiting entries
- insight previews after enough entries exist
- feeling of a growing personal archive

Avoid relying on spammy notification tactics.

---

## Distribution Ideas

### Low-Cost Options
- TikTok / Reels with atmospheric product clips
- Reddit communities around dreams and journaling
- Product Hunt if product polish is strong
- design and productivity communities
- personal developer journey content

### Medium-Term
- partnerships with journaling creators
- sleep or reflection newsletters
- curated short-form content around dream rituals and memory capture

---

## Messaging Themes

Good themes:
- your dreams disappear fast — capture them beautifully
- revisit symbols and moods over time
- a private archive for your dream life
- dream journaling that feels premium and calm

Avoid:
- exaggerated therapeutic claims
- mystical certainty claims
- fake scientific authority

---

## Launch Sequence

### Pre-Launch
- finalize identity
- create waitlist or landing page
- collect feedback on visuals and core flow
- test messaging

### Soft Launch
- release MVP to a small audience
- observe first-week retention
- collect qualitative feedback
- refine onboarding and reminders

### Public Launch
- release with polished assets
- post content showing real experience
- highlight design and privacy
- push first premium differentiators only if product is ready

---

## Go-To-Market Summary

The app is most likely to stand out if launch messaging focuses on:
- beauty
- privacy
- emotional depth
- speed of capture
- the feeling that old dreams become more meaningful over time
